Wick Candles

Wick candles are candles that use a wick—a slender piece of material, typically made of braided cotton, hemp, or other natural fibers—to create and sustain the flame when lit. The wick acts as a conduit for the wax, drawing it up into the flame where it is consumed to maintain the light and heat.

The wick's design and size are essential for the proper functioning of a candle. A wick that is too small may not burn properly, while one that is too large can lead to excessive soot or an uneven burn. Different types of wicks, such as flat, braided, or twisted, also affect the burning characteristics of the candle.

Wick candles come in various forms, including scented, decorative, and functional candles. The wick is typically placed at the center of the candle, and as the wax melts, it creates a warm, inviting ambiance. Many wick candles are used for relaxation, aromatherapy, or simply to add warmth and light to a space.
